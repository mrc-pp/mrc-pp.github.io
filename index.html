Overview

MRCPP targets large, non-convex ROIs with obstacles and no-fly zones, focusing on turn-efficient, feasible, and workload-balanced multi-robot coverage.

Minimum-turn swath generation

Finds an optimal sweep orientation using a minimum-area enclosing rectangle (rotating calipers), then generates parallel swaths aligned with the principal axis.

Obstacle-aware connectivity

Builds a visibility roadmap (VRM) inside a buffered feasible region and uses it to replace obstructed straight-line transitions with collision-free detours.

Balanced multi-robot allocation

Uses a modified multiple Traveling Salesman Problem (mTSP) formulation to allocate swaths across robots and reduce mission completion time.

Method

A clean pipeline view of the full MRCPP stack.

1

Optimal sweep orientation

Compute the minimum-area enclosing rectangle (via rotating calipers) to align coverage with the ROI‚Äôs principal axis.

2

Minimum-turn swaths

Generate parallel candidate lines and clip them against the ROI to obtain feasible swath segments.

3

Headland buffers

Offset ROI inward and obstacles outward to guarantee turning clearance and prevent boundary violations.

4

VRM connectivity

Create a visibility roadmap in the buffered region and compute shortest feasible transitions between disjoint swaths.

5

mTSP allocation + execution

Allocate ordered swaths across robots and generate executable routes with alternating-edge traversal and VRM refinement.

Results

MRCPP reduces turns and energy while remaining computationally efficient and scalable.

Benchmarks

Evaluate across multiple environments (e.g., Cape, Complex, Island, Rect, Simple) using consistent settings and metrics: computation time, energy (trajectory-free and trajectory-based), and path length.

  • Fast planning time (seconds-level in most cases)
  • Lower average energy per robot
  • Competitive path length, strong scalability with increased fleet size

Scalability

Scale the number of AAVs while maintaining low computation time and balanced energy usage per robot.

EAMCMP benchmark coverage paths

Compared to MRCPP, EAMCMP consistently exhibits higher energy consumption and longer execution paths across benchmark environments. The absence of an explicit minimum-turn swath orientation leads to frequent reorientation maneuvers and increased ferrying distance, particularly in elongated or structured regions. Consequently, EAMCMP underperforms MRCPP in both energy efficiency and mission completion time.

AAV experiment (DJI Mavic Air)

Upload planned paths to mission software and execute with two AAVs over a polygonal ROI with a no-fly zone.

  • Two AAVs execute assigned swaths concurrently
  • Paths follow minimum-turn orientation

ASV experiment (coastal bay)

Two ASVs equipped with in-situ sensing execute coverage paths while operators monitor live video and path overlays.

  • Real-time monitoring interface
  • Feasible transitions maintained within the operable region
