Abstract

We present MRCPP, a framework for multi-robot coverage path planning in large, non-convex Regions of Interest (ROIs) containing obstacles and no-fly zones. MRCPP first determines an optimal sweep orientation using a minimum-area enclosing rectangle computed via a rotating-calipers procedure. With this orientation, a minimum-turn swath-generation algorithm produces parallel coverage lines clipped against obstacle-free space and ordered along the field’s minor axis to improve continuity and reduce turn transitions. We expand both the outer ROI boundary and internal exclusion zones using headland buffers to guarantee clearance for turning maneuvers. A visibility-roadmap (VRM) provides connectivity between disjoint swath segments via feasible transitions entirely inside the buffered region. Finally, a modified mTSP formulation allocates ordered swaths to robots to balance workloads and reduce mission time. Simulations and real-world experiments show improvements in turns, energy, and computation time, with videos available at mrc-pp.github.io.

Overview & Contributions

MRCPP integrates analytic geometry, graph-based connectivity, and optimization-based allocation to produce efficient coverage paths for non-convex ROIs with obstacles and no-fly zones.

Key Ideas

  • Minimum-area sweep orientation: Rotating calipers compute the minimum-area enclosing rectangle to set a globally consistent sweep direction.
  • Minimum-turn swath generation: Parallel swaths aligned with the rectangle’s major axis are clipped to the ROI and ordered along the minor axis.
  • Headland buffers: Inward offset of the ROI and outward expansion of exclusion zones create a safe operable region for turns.
  • VRM connectivity: A visibility-roadmap graph connects disjoint swaths using collision-free detours within the buffered region.
  • Workload-balanced allocation: A modified mTSP assigns swaths to minimize the maximum robot path length (mission time proxy).

Benchmark Results

We evaluate MRCPP on multiple benchmark environments (e.g., Cape, Complex, Island, Simple, Rectangle) with obstacles/no-fly zones, reporting computation time, energy (trajectory-free and trajectory-based), and path length. We also test scalability in a fixed NFZ-constrained complex environment as the number of robots increases.

Real-World Field Experiments

We validate MRCPP with two real-world deployments: (i) aerial coverage using two DJI Mavic Air AAVs (paths uploaded and executed via Litchi), and (ii) aquatic coverage using two ASVs equipped with YSI EXO2 sondes for in situ sensing. The executed coverage paths and the no-fly zone are visualized in the experiment interface and overlaid on satellite imagery.
